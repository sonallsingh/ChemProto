GINGEROL EXTRACTION: STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

Optimized laboratory-scale method using ethanol to maximize 6-gingerol yield while minimizing thermal degradation of the β-hydroxy ketone.

01

Washing & Preparation

Wash 500 g fresh ginger rhizomes thoroughly. Peel and slice into 2–3 mm thin pieces or grate to maximize surface area for solvent penetration.

02

Drying & Grinding

Dry slices at low temperature (35–40°C) for 4–6 h to reduce moisture <10%. Grind to 60–80 mesh powder. Low temperature prevents conversion of 6-gingerol to 6-shogaol.

03

Ethanol Extraction

Macerate or reflux powder with 95% ethanol (1:8 to 1:10 w/v) at 50–60°C for 3–8 hours. Ethanol is the best solvent for high 6-gingerol recovery (typically 7–12 mg/g dry weight).

04

Filtration & Concentration

Filter through Whatman No.1 paper. Concentrate filtrate using rotary evaporator at ≤40°C under reduced pressure to avoid degradation of thermolabile 6-gingerol.

05

Drying & Yield

Air-dry or freeze-dry to obtain thick extract. Expected yield: 8–12% w/w. HPLC assay typically shows 6-gingerol content ≥8 mg/g.

✅

Final Gingerol-Rich Extract

Store in amber glass. Label with batch number, date, and 6-gingerol assay result.

Note: 6-Gingerol is thermolabile. Prolonged high temperature (>60°C) causes dehydration to 6-shogaol, which is more pungent but may alter bioactivity profile.

QUALITY CONTROL PARAMETERS

  • 6-Gingerol content (HPLC)≥ 8 mg/g
  • Total gingerols + shogaols12–22 mg/g
  • pH of extract4.5 – 6.0
  • Microbial load (TPC)< 100 CFU/g
  • Residual ethanol< 5000 ppm

STORAGE & PACKAGING

Store in airtight amber glass bottles at 4–8°C away from light. Shelf life: 12–18 months. Use child-resistant caps and proper batch labeling.

KEY CHEMICAL REACTION & MECHANISM

Dehydration of 6-Gingerol to 6-Shogaol

6-Gingerol (C₁₇H₂₆O₄) → 6-Shogaol (C₁₇H₂₄O₃) + H₂O
Acid/heat-catalyzed β-hydroxy ketone dehydration

This reaction occurs during drying or high-temperature extraction. 6-Shogaol is often more biologically potent but less abundant in fresh ginger extracts.
